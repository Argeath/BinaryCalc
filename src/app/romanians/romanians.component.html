Roman & Arabic numerals conversion

Roman numerals originated in ancient Rome and remained the usual way of writing numbers throughout Europe well into the Late Middle Ages.

Numbers in Roman system are represented by combinations of letters from the Latin alphabet. Roman numerals, as used today, are based on seven symbols:
Symbol Value
I 1
V 5
X 10
L 50
C 100
D 500
M 1000

Symbols are placed from left to right in order of value, starting with the largest.
However, in a few specific cases, to avoid four characters being repeated in succession (such as IIII or XXXX), subtractive notation is used, as in this table:
Symbol Value
IV 4
IX 9
XL 40
XC 90
CD 400
CM 900

For example - numbers from 1 to 10:
I, II, III, IV, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X.
